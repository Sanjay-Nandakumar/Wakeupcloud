  • The enigma of Adjusted R Squared in regression analysis

    25 July 2020

    Regression analysis is one of the most fundamental but commanding machine learning technique which is still predominant and made a way for many advanced kinds of research in the industry. Although there are a handful of advanced regression techniques already serving the purpose of predicting the continuous variables like bagging, boosting, support vectors, etc, linear regression principles remain as the first choice for most of the researchers if the data perpetuates a rectilinear fashion when represented on a multidimensional space. One of the most widely used evaluation metrics for the linear regression models is R squared aka Coefficient of determination. R squared is considered as a goodness of fit metric which in most of the time ranges around 0 to 1. Higher the value of R Squared examined as higher the coherence and predictive ability of the model. But as like most of the other evaluation metrics in machine learning, R Squared has also some restraints which make it to give imprecise indications sometimes by signifying an indigent model with an extremely high value. In this article, we will discuss the calculation procedure of R Squared, it’s limitations, and how these limitations can be overridden using an advanced evaluation metric called Adjusted R Squared.
